Jorge's Guide to Building Better Websites

Building a website can be very intimidating. The following tips could be helpful in getting you started!

1- Consider your color choices

Do:

Pick a well thought-out color pallete. There are great guides online to help you with pre-picked arrangements.

Don't:

Use an excessive amount of color. Some don't work well together, and will make your webpage harder to see overall.

2- Inspect your images carefully

Do:

Select a high quality, well saturated image. Align it well within the context of your webpage, and size it appropriately.

Look at the cute, HD puppy. He deserves to be the focal point, therefore his image is nice and large.

Don't:

Use low quality images that take away from the aesthetic. Poor positioning and sizing can ruin the purpose of the image.

Look at this still cute, but poorly formatted, low picture quality puppy. He could be so much better.

3- Think about the Flow

Do:

Think about how someone should navigate your site.

What information will become available as they scroll?

How will they access different sections?

How will you organize information so that people can focus in on what they care about?

Don't:

Clump everything together.

Webpages that are hard to navigate typically have images, links, text, animations, etc. all mixed together with no sense of organization.

This can be overwhelming to the viewer of your webpage.

4- Visual clutter (or lack of it)

Do: Limit yourself to 1-2 ideas at a time on screen. This will allow people to focus in on whatever is on the page.

Don't: Cram a ton of information into every section.

Excessive text makes people lose interest quickly.
